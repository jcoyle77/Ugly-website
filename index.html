The first game we're going to be talking about is Space Invaders. This is a classic, even for the retro gaming world. It was initally released in the year 1978. Gameplay involved the player moving a spaceship up, down, left and right, while pressing buttons to shoot rows of aliens who were firing at the player. Clearing all the rows of enemy aliens would allow you to move on to the next level of the game. In the span of 4 years, the game had made developers $3.8 billion. Considering this was in the 80's, if adjusting for inflation it would be a lot more money by todays standards.

This original arcade version of Mario Brothers was released in the year 1983. This first iteration was the basis for launching a whole series of super successful line of games. They originally made their first appearance in the first version of Donkey Kong two years prior, in the year 1981. The game's main characters are two Italian plumbers, Mario and his brother Luigi. The acutal gameplay includes Mario and Luigi investigating the sewers of New York after enemy monsters started appearing there. As one might expect, as the levels increase so does the difficulty.
